# Art Deco Architecture

## Overview

Art Deco is an architectural and decorative style that originated in France in the 1920s and flourished internationally through the 1930s and 1940s. It combines modernist styles with fine craftsmanship and rich materials, featuring bold geometric forms, lavish ornamentation, and vibrant colors.

## Characteristics

- Geometric shapes: zigzags, chevrons, sunbursts, and stepped forms
- Symmetry: strong central axis with balanced composition
- Rich materials: marble, chrome, brass, lacquer, exotic woods
- Stylized decoration: abstracted natural forms (sunflowers, fountains, animals)
- Vertical emphasis: setback towers and spires reaching skyward
- Bold colors: gold, silver, black, and bright accents

## Famous Art Deco Buildings

- Chrysler Building, New York (1930): 319 meters, stainless steel crown with triangular windows, eagle gargoyles. Considered the finest Art Deco skyscraper.
- Empire State Building, New York (1931): 443 meters, limestone and granite facade with aluminum and chrome details
- Rockefeller Center, New York (1930-1939): complex of 14 buildings with murals, sculptures, and gardens
- Palais de Chaillot, Paris (1937): curved wings framing the Trocadéro gardens
- Marine Building, Vancouver (1930): terra cotta decoration depicting marine life
- Napier, New Zealand: entire city center rebuilt in Art Deco after a 1931 earthquake

## Historical Context

Art Deco emerged after World War I during a period of rapid industrialization and social change. It celebrated modernity, technology, speed, and luxury. The style was named after the 1925 Exposition Internationale des Arts Décoratifs et Industriels Modernes in Paris. The Great Depression and World War II ended the style's dominance, as austerity made its lavishness inappropriate.

## Legacy

Art Deco influenced subsequent styles including Streamline Moderne and mid-century modern design. Many Art Deco buildings are now protected landmarks. The style has experienced periodic revivals, particularly in interior design, jewelry, and graphic design.
