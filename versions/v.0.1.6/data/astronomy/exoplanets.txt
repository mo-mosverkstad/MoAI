# Exoplanets

## Definition

An exoplanet is a planet that orbits a star other than the Sun. The first confirmed exoplanet orbiting a Sun-like star was 51 Pegasi b, discovered in 1995 by Michel Mayor and Didier Queloz (Nobel Prize 2019). As of 2024, over 5,500 exoplanets have been confirmed.

## Detection Methods

- Radial velocity (Doppler): measures the star's wobble caused by an orbiting planet's gravitational pull. Detects the planet's minimum mass and orbital period.
- Transit method: measures the tiny dimming of a star as a planet passes in front of it. Reveals the planet's size and orbital period. The Kepler space telescope discovered over 2,600 planets using this method.
- Direct imaging: photographing the planet directly (extremely difficult due to the star's glare). Works best for large, young planets far from their star.
- Gravitational microlensing: a planet's gravity bends light from a background star.
- Astrometry: measuring the star's precise position shift due to a planet's gravity.

## Types of Exoplanets

- Hot Jupiters: gas giants orbiting very close to their star (orbital periods of days). Example: 51 Pegasi b.
- Super-Earths: rocky planets 1-10 times Earth's mass. No equivalent in our Solar System.
- Mini-Neptunes: planets between Earth and Neptune in size, likely with thick atmospheres.
- Earth-like planets: rocky planets in the habitable zone where liquid water could exist.
- Rogue planets: planets not bound to any star, drifting through interstellar space.

## The Habitable Zone

The habitable zone (Goldilocks zone) is the region around a star where temperatures allow liquid water on a planet's surface:

- Too close: water evaporates (Venus)
- Too far: water freezes (Mars is at the outer edge)
- Just right: liquid water possible (Earth)
- The habitable zone depends on the star's luminosity and the planet's atmosphere

## Notable Exoplanets

- Proxima Centauri b: nearest known exoplanet (4.24 light-years), in the habitable zone of a red dwarf
- TRAPPIST-1 system: seven Earth-sized planets, three in the habitable zone (39 light-years away)
- Kepler-452b: "Earth's cousin," similar size and orbital period in a Sun-like star's habitable zone
- HD 209458 b: first exoplanet observed transiting its star, first with atmosphere detected

## Search for Life

- Biosignatures: atmospheric gases (oxygen, methane, ozone) that could indicate biological activity
- James Webb Space Telescope (launched 2021): can analyze exoplanet atmospheres for biosignatures
- Drake equation: estimates the number of communicating civilizations in our galaxy
- Fermi paradox: if intelligent life is common, why haven't we detected it?
