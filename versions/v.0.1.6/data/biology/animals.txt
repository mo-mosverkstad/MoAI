# Animals

Animals are multicellular organisms belonging to kingdom Animalia. They exist in diverse environments ranging from the deep ocean to forests, deserts, and polar regions.

Multicellular organisms distinguished by movement, consumption of organic material, complex behavior, and nervous systems.

## Mammals

Mammals are warm-blooded vertebrates characterized by:
- Fur or hair
- Mammary glands
- Live birth (mostly)

Examples:
- Elephants
- Tigers
- Bats
- Whales

## Birds

Birds are feathered vertebrates with beaks known for:
- Feathers
- Hollow bones
- Beaks
- Ability to fly (most species)

## Reptiles, Amphibians, and Fish

Reptiles are cold-blooded animals with scales and include crocodiles, turtles, lizards, and snakes. Amphibians such as frogs and salamanders live both in water and on land, with a life cycle that includes metamorphosis. Fish represent the largest group of vertebrates and are aquatic animals that breathe through gills.

## Behavioral Adaptations

Animals exhibit complex behaviors:
- Migration
- Hibernation
- Social organization (e.g., bees, wolves)
- Communication via sound, scent, movement
