# Taxation

## Definition

Taxation is the process by which governments collect revenue from individuals and businesses to fund public services and infrastructure. Taxes are compulsory contributions — failure to pay is punishable by law.

## Types of Taxes

- Income tax: levied on earnings from employment, investments, and business profits. Most countries use progressive rates (higher income = higher rate).
- Corporate tax: levied on business profits. Rates vary widely (Ireland 12.5%, US 21%, Japan 30%).
- Sales tax / VAT: levied on purchases of goods and services. VAT (Value Added Tax) is collected at each stage of production.
- Property tax: levied on real estate ownership, based on assessed value.
- Capital gains tax: levied on profits from selling assets (stocks, property).
- Estate/inheritance tax: levied on wealth transferred after death.
- Excise tax: levied on specific goods (alcohol, tobacco, fuel) — often called "sin taxes."
- Payroll tax: funds social insurance programs (Social Security, Medicare in the US).

## Tax Principles

- Ability to pay: those with higher income should pay more (progressive taxation)
- Benefit principle: those who benefit from public services should pay for them
- Horizontal equity: people in similar situations should pay similar taxes
- Vertical equity: people in different situations should pay different amounts
- Efficiency: taxes should minimize distortion of economic decisions
- Simplicity: tax systems should be easy to understand and administer

## Progressive vs. Regressive

- Progressive tax: rate increases with income (income tax with brackets)
- Proportional (flat) tax: same rate for all income levels
- Regressive tax: takes a larger percentage from low-income earners (sales tax, lottery tickets)

## Tax Revenue Usage

Governments use tax revenue for:

- Defense and security
- Healthcare and social services
- Education
- Infrastructure (roads, bridges, public transit)
- Debt service (interest on government borrowing)
- Social safety nets (unemployment insurance, pensions)

## Tax Avoidance and Evasion

- Tax avoidance: legal strategies to minimize tax liability (deductions, credits, offshore structures)
- Tax evasion: illegal failure to pay taxes owed (hiding income, false deductions)
- Tax havens: jurisdictions with very low or zero tax rates (Cayman Islands, Luxembourg, Bermuda)
- OECD global minimum tax (2021): agreement for a 15% minimum corporate tax rate across 140 countries
