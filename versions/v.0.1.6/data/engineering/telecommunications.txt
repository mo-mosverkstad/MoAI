# Telecommunications

## Definition

Telecommunications is the transmission of information over significant distances by electronic means. It encompasses telephone, radio, television, internet, and satellite communications — the infrastructure that connects the modern world.

## History

- 1837: Samuel Morse demonstrates the electric telegraph
- 1876: Alexander Graham Bell patents the telephone
- 1895: Guglielmo Marconi demonstrates wireless telegraphy (radio)
- 1927: First transatlantic telephone call (radio link)
- 1956: First transatlantic telephone cable (TAT-1)
- 1962: Telstar — first active communications satellite
- 1969: ARPANET — precursor to the internet
- 1983: First cellular phone call (Motorola DynaTAC)
- 1991: GSM digital mobile standard launched in Europe
- 2007: iPhone launches the smartphone era
- 2019: First 5G networks deployed

## Mobile Generations

- 1G (1980s): analog voice only (AMPS)
- 2G (1991): digital voice, SMS text messaging (GSM, CDMA)
- 3G (2001): mobile internet, video calls (UMTS, CDMA2000). Up to 2 Mbps.
- 4G/LTE (2009): high-speed mobile broadband, streaming video. Up to 100 Mbps.
- 5G (2019): ultra-fast, low latency, massive device connectivity. Up to 10 Gbps. Enables IoT, autonomous vehicles, remote surgery.

## How Mobile Networks Work

- Base stations (cell towers): provide radio coverage over a geographic area (cell)
- Cells: geographic areas served by one base station (range: 100m to 35km depending on technology)
- Handoff: seamless transfer of a call between cells as the user moves
- Spectrum: radio frequencies allocated by governments for different services
- MIMO (Multiple Input Multiple Output): multiple antennas increase capacity and speed
- Beamforming: directing radio signals toward specific users rather than broadcasting in all directions

## Fiber Optics

- Light pulses travel through thin glass fibers at near-light speed
- Capacity: a single fiber can carry terabits per second
- Submarine cables: over 400 cables on the ocean floor carry 95% of intercontinental data
- Total length of submarine cables: over 1.3 million km
- Advantages over copper: higher bandwidth, longer distances, immune to electromagnetic interference

## Satellite Communications

- Geostationary orbit (GEO): 35,786 km altitude, appears stationary (TV broadcasting, weather)
- Medium Earth orbit (MEO): GPS satellites at ~20,200 km
- Low Earth orbit (LEO): 160-2,000 km, low latency (Starlink: 5,000+ satellites for global internet)
- Latency: GEO ~600ms round trip, LEO ~20-40ms round trip
