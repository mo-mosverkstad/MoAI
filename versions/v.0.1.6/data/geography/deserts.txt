# Deserts

## Definition

A desert is a region that receives less than 250 mm (10 inches) of precipitation per year. Deserts cover approximately 33% of Earth's land surface. They are not necessarily hot — Antarctica is technically the world's largest desert.

## Types of Deserts

- Subtropical deserts: caused by descending dry air at ~30° latitude (Sahara, Arabian, Kalahari)
- Rain shadow deserts: mountains block moisture-carrying winds (Gobi, Patagonian, Great Basin)
- Coastal deserts: cold ocean currents cool air, preventing rain (Atacama, Namib)
- Polar deserts: extremely cold air holds very little moisture (Antarctica, Arctic)
- Interior deserts: far from ocean moisture sources (Gobi, Taklamakan)

## Major Deserts

- Sahara (Africa): world's largest hot desert, 9.2 million km² (roughly the size of the United States). Temperatures reach 58°C. Only 25% is sand dunes; most is rocky plateau.
- Antarctic Desert: largest overall desert, 14.2 million km². Average temperature -49°C. Receives only 51 mm precipitation annually.
- Arabian Desert: 2.3 million km², covers most of the Arabian Peninsula. Contains the Rub' al Khali (Empty Quarter), the largest continuous sand desert.
- Gobi (Asia): 1.3 million km², cold desert in Mongolia and China. Temperatures range from -40°C to 45°C.
- Atacama (Chile): driest non-polar desert. Some weather stations have never recorded rain. Used as a Mars analog by NASA.

## Desert Adaptations

Plants:

- Cacti: store water in stems, spines reduce water loss and deter herbivores
- Deep roots: mesquite roots can reach 50 meters deep to access groundwater
- Ephemeral plants: complete entire life cycle in weeks after rare rainfall
- Waxy coatings: reduce evaporation from leaf surfaces

Animals:

- Camels: tolerate 25% body water loss, store fat (not water) in humps, can drink 100 liters in 10 minutes
- Kangaroo rat: never drinks water — obtains all moisture from metabolizing dry seeds
- Fennec fox: enormous ears radiate heat, nocturnal to avoid daytime temperatures
- Sidewinder rattlesnake: moves sideways to minimize contact with hot sand

## Desertification

The expansion of desert conditions into previously non-desert areas:

- Affects approximately 3.6 billion hectares (25% of Earth's land)
- Causes: overgrazing, deforestation, unsustainable farming, climate change
- Sahel region (Africa): southern edge of Sahara advancing southward
- Affects 250 million people directly, threatens 1 billion more
- Solutions: sustainable land management, reforestation (Great Green Wall of Africa), improved irrigation
