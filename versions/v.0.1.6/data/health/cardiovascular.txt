# Cardiovascular Disease

## Overview

Cardiovascular disease (CVD) is the leading cause of death globally, responsible for approximately 17.9 million deaths per year (32% of all deaths). CVD refers to conditions affecting the heart and blood vessels.

## Types

- Coronary artery disease (CAD): narrowing of arteries supplying the heart due to plaque buildup (atherosclerosis). Can cause angina (chest pain) or heart attack (myocardial infarction).
- Stroke: interruption of blood supply to the brain. Ischemic stroke (blocked artery, 87%) or hemorrhagic stroke (burst blood vessel, 13%).
- Heart failure: the heart cannot pump blood efficiently enough to meet the body's needs. Affects approximately 64 million people worldwide.
- Arrhythmias: abnormal heart rhythms (atrial fibrillation is the most common, affecting 33 million people).
- Peripheral artery disease: narrowed arteries reduce blood flow to limbs.
- Aortic aneurysm: weakening and bulging of the aorta wall.

## Risk Factors

Modifiable:

- High blood pressure (hypertension): the single largest risk factor, affects 1.3 billion people
- High cholesterol: LDL ("bad") cholesterol builds up in artery walls
- Smoking: doubles cardiovascular risk, damages blood vessel lining
- Diabetes: high blood sugar damages blood vessels over time
- Obesity: especially abdominal fat, increases strain on the heart
- Physical inactivity: regular exercise reduces risk by 30-50%
- Unhealthy diet: high in saturated fat, salt, and processed foods
- Excessive alcohol: raises blood pressure and triglycerides

Non-modifiable:

- Age: risk increases significantly after 55 (men) or 65 (women)
- Family history: genetic predisposition
- Sex: men have higher risk at younger ages; women's risk increases after menopause

## Heart Attack

A heart attack occurs when a coronary artery is completely blocked, cutting off blood supply to heart muscle:

Symptoms:

- Chest pain or pressure (may radiate to arm, jaw, or back)
- Shortness of breath
- Cold sweat, nausea, lightheadedness
- Women may have atypical symptoms (fatigue, indigestion, back pain)

Treatment: call emergency services immediately. Aspirin, clot-dissolving drugs, angioplasty with stent placement, or bypass surgery.

## Prevention

- Regular exercise: at least 150 minutes of moderate activity per week
- Healthy diet: Mediterranean diet reduces CVD risk by 30%
- Blood pressure control: target below 130/80 mmHg
- Cholesterol management: statins reduce heart attack risk by 25-35%
- Smoking cessation: risk drops significantly within 1-2 years of quitting
- Weight management: maintaining healthy BMI (18.5-24.9)
