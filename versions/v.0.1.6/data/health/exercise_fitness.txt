# Exercise and Physical Fitness

## Benefits of Exercise

Regular physical activity is one of the most important things people can do for their health:

- Reduces risk of cardiovascular disease by 30-50%
- Reduces risk of Type 2 diabetes by 40-60%
- Reduces risk of certain cancers (colon, breast) by 20-30%
- Strengthens bones and muscles, reducing osteoporosis risk
- Improves mental health: reduces depression and anxiety symptoms
- Enhances cognitive function and reduces dementia risk
- Improves sleep quality
- Increases lifespan by 3-7 years on average
- Helps maintain healthy weight

## Types of Exercise

- Aerobic (cardio): sustained activity that raises heart rate (running, swimming, cycling, dancing). Improves cardiovascular fitness and endurance.
- Resistance (strength) training: working muscles against resistance (weights, bodyweight exercises, resistance bands). Builds muscle mass and bone density.
- Flexibility: stretching exercises that improve range of motion (yoga, Pilates, static stretching).
- Balance: exercises that improve stability and prevent falls (tai chi, single-leg stands). Especially important for older adults.
- High-intensity interval training (HIIT): alternating short bursts of intense exercise with recovery periods. Time-efficient and effective.

## Exercise Recommendations

World Health Organization guidelines for adults (18-64):

- At least 150-300 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic activity per week, OR
- At least 75-150 minutes of vigorous-intensity aerobic activity per week
- Muscle-strengthening activities involving all major muscle groups on 2+ days per week
- Limit sedentary time; any physical activity is better than none

## Exercise Physiology

What happens during exercise:

- Heart rate increases to deliver more oxygen to working muscles
- Breathing rate increases to take in more oxygen and expel CO₂
- Blood flow redirected from digestive organs to muscles
- Glycogen (stored glucose) broken down for energy
- After glycogen depletion, fat becomes primary fuel source
- Endorphins released, producing "runner's high"
- Muscle fibers sustain micro-tears that repair stronger (adaptation)

## Overtraining

Too much exercise without adequate recovery can be harmful:

- Overtraining syndrome: persistent fatigue, decreased performance, mood disturbance
- Increased injury risk: stress fractures, tendinitis, muscle strains
- Immune suppression: heavy training temporarily reduces immune function
- Rest and recovery are essential parts of any training program
- Progressive overload: gradually increasing intensity prevents injury
