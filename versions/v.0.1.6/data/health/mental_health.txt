# Mental Health

## Definition

Mental health is a state of well-being in which an individual realizes their own abilities, can cope with normal stresses of life, can work productively, and can contribute to their community. Mental health is not merely the absence of mental illness — it is a positive state of psychological functioning.

## Global Burden

- Approximately 1 in 8 people worldwide live with a mental health condition (970 million people)
- Depression and anxiety are the most common conditions
- Mental health conditions account for 1 in 6 years lived with disability globally
- Suicide claims approximately 700,000 lives per year (second leading cause of death among 15-29 year olds)
- Treatment gap: in low-income countries, over 75% of people with mental health conditions receive no treatment

## Factors Affecting Mental Health

Biological:

- Genetics: many conditions have heritable components (30-80% depending on condition)
- Brain chemistry: neurotransmitter imbalances (serotonin, dopamine, norepinephrine)
- Hormones: thyroid dysfunction, cortisol dysregulation
- Physical health: chronic illness, pain, sleep disorders

Psychological:

- Childhood experiences: trauma, neglect, attachment disruption
- Cognitive patterns: negative thinking styles, rumination
- Coping skills: ability to manage stress and adversity
- Self-esteem and identity

Social:

- Relationships: social support, loneliness, conflict
- Socioeconomic status: poverty, unemployment, housing insecurity
- Discrimination: racism, sexism, homophobia, stigma
- Life events: bereavement, divorce, job loss, migration

## Stigma

Mental health stigma remains a major barrier to treatment:

- Public stigma: negative attitudes and discrimination from others
- Self-stigma: internalized shame and reduced self-esteem
- Structural stigma: institutional policies that disadvantage people with mental illness
- Consequences: delayed help-seeking, social isolation, reduced employment opportunities
- Anti-stigma strategies: education, contact with people who have lived experience, media representation

## Promoting Mental Health

- Social connections: maintaining meaningful relationships
- Physical activity: regular exercise improves mood and reduces anxiety
- Sleep hygiene: consistent sleep schedule, 7-9 hours for adults
- Stress management: mindfulness, relaxation techniques, time management
- Purpose and meaning: engaging in valued activities and goals
- Professional help: therapy and medication when needed — seeking help is a sign of strength
- Limiting alcohol and avoiding drugs
- Digital wellness: managing screen time and social media use
