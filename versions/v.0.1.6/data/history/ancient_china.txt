# Ancient China

## Overview

Chinese civilization is one of the world's oldest continuous civilizations, with written records dating back over 3,500 years. Ancient China produced numerous inventions and philosophical traditions that shaped East Asian culture and influenced the entire world.

## Major Dynasties

- Shang Dynasty (1600-1046 BC): earliest confirmed dynasty, oracle bone script (earliest Chinese writing), bronze casting
- Zhou Dynasty (1046-256 BC): longest dynasty, feudal system, Confucius and Laozi lived during this period
- Qin Dynasty (221-206 BC): first unified Chinese empire under Qin Shi Huang, Great Wall begun, terracotta army, standardized writing/weights/measures
- Han Dynasty (206 BC - 220 AD): golden age comparable to Roman Empire, Silk Road trade, paper invented, Confucianism becomes state ideology
- Tang Dynasty (618-907 AD): cosmopolitan golden age, poetry flourished (Li Bai, Du Fu), Buddhism spread widely
- Song Dynasty (960-1279): technological innovation (printing, gunpowder, compass), economic prosperity
- Ming Dynasty (1368-1644): Great Wall rebuilt in current form, Forbidden City constructed, Zheng He's maritime expeditions
- Qing Dynasty (1644-1912): last imperial dynasty, Manchu rule, declined in 19th century

## The Four Great Inventions

Chinese inventions that transformed world civilization:

- Paper (105 AD): Cai Lun standardized papermaking. Reached the Islamic world by 751 AD and Europe by the 12th century.
- Printing (868 AD woodblock, 1040 AD movable type): Bi Sheng invented movable type 400 years before Gutenberg.
- Gunpowder (9th century): originally used for fireworks, later for weapons. Reached Europe by the 13th century.
- Compass (11th century): magnetic compass for navigation. Enabled the Age of Exploration.

## Philosophy

- Confucianism: social harmony through proper relationships, education, and virtue (ren, li, xiao)
- Daoism: harmony with nature, wu wei (non-action), simplicity
- Legalism: strict laws and punishments for social order (influenced Qin dynasty)
- Buddhism: arrived from India via the Silk Road (1st century AD), became deeply integrated into Chinese culture

## The Great Wall

The Great Wall of China stretches approximately 21,196 km across northern China. Built over 2,000 years by multiple dynasties to protect against nomadic invasions. The most well-known sections were built during the Ming Dynasty (1368-1644) using brick and stone. It is visible from low Earth orbit but not from the Moon (contrary to popular myth).

## The Silk Road

The Silk Road was a network of trade routes connecting China to the Mediterranean (active from 130 BC to 1453 AD):

- Traded goods: silk, spices, tea, porcelain, gold, silver, glass, horses
- Spread ideas: Buddhism, Islam, Christianity, technology, art
- Length: approximately 6,400 km from Chang'an (Xi'an) to Constantinople
- Named by German geographer Ferdinand von Richthofen in 1877
