# Ancient Egypt

## Overview

Ancient Egypt was a civilization in northeastern Africa concentrated along the lower Nile River. It lasted for over 3,000 years, from approximately 3100 BC to 30 BC when it was conquered by Rome. Ancient Egypt is renowned for its monumental architecture, art, writing system, and advances in medicine and mathematics.

## The Nile River

The Nile was the lifeblood of ancient Egyptian civilization. Its annual flooding deposited rich silt on the surrounding farmland, enabling agriculture in an otherwise desert environment. The Nile flows north for 6,650 km, making it one of the longest rivers in the world. Ancient Egyptians developed irrigation systems to control and distribute floodwaters.

## Periods of Egyptian History

- Early Dynastic Period (3100-2686 BC): unification of Upper and Lower Egypt under King Narmer
- Old Kingdom (2686-2181 BC): the Age of Pyramids, including the Great Pyramid of Giza
- Middle Kingdom (2055-1650 BC): cultural renaissance, expansion into Nubia
- New Kingdom (1550-1069 BC): Egypt's golden age, including Hatshepsut, Akhenaten, Tutankhamun, and Ramesses II
- Late Period (664-332 BC): foreign domination by Assyrians, Persians
- Ptolemaic Period (332-30 BC): Greek rule following Alexander the Great's conquest, ending with Cleopatra VII

## The Pyramids

The pyramids were royal tombs built during the Old Kingdom:

- Step Pyramid of Djoser (2667 BC): the first pyramid, designed by architect Imhotep
- Great Pyramid of Giza (2560 BC): built for Pharaoh Khufu, originally 146.5 meters tall, constructed from approximately 2.3 million limestone blocks averaging 2.5 tons each
- Construction methods: ramps, levers, and organized labor forces of approximately 20,000-30,000 workers (not slaves, as once believed)

## Hieroglyphics

Egyptian hieroglyphics were a formal writing system using pictorial symbols. The system contained over 700 distinct signs. Hieroglyphics were used for religious texts and monumental inscriptions. The Rosetta Stone (196 BC), discovered in 1799, enabled Jean-François Champollion to decipher hieroglyphics in 1822 by comparing them with Greek text.

## Religion and Afterlife

Ancient Egyptians believed in an afterlife and practiced mummification to preserve the body:

- Major gods: Ra (sun god), Osiris (god of the dead), Isis (goddess of magic), Anubis (god of embalming)
- Mummification: a 70-day process involving organ removal, desiccation with natron salt, and wrapping in linen
- Book of the Dead: collection of spells to guide the deceased through the underworld
- Judgment of the dead: the heart was weighed against the feather of Ma'at (truth and justice)
