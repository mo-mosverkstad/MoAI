# The Cold War

## Overview

The Cold War (1947-1991) was a geopolitical rivalry between the United States and the Soviet Union and their respective allies. It was called "cold" because the two superpowers never fought each other directly, instead competing through proxy wars, nuclear arms races, espionage, propaganda, and the Space Race.

## Origins

- Ideological conflict: capitalism and democracy (US) vs. communism and one-party rule (USSR)
- Post-WWII division: Europe divided into Western (US-aligned) and Eastern (Soviet-aligned) blocs
- Iron Curtain: Winston Churchill's 1946 term for the division of Europe
- Truman Doctrine (1947): US policy to contain the spread of communism
- Marshall Plan (1948): US economic aid to rebuild Western Europe ($13 billion)
- Berlin Blockade (1948-1949): Soviet blockade of West Berlin, broken by Allied airlift

## Major Events

- Korean War (1950-1953): first major proxy war, ended in stalemate at the 38th parallel
- Hungarian Revolution (1956): Soviet tanks crush anti-communist uprising
- Cuban Missile Crisis (1962): closest the world came to nuclear war. Soviet missiles in Cuba, 13-day standoff, resolved by diplomacy.
- Vietnam War (1955-1975): US intervention against communist North Vietnam, ended in US withdrawal
- Prague Spring (1968): Soviet invasion ends Czechoslovak liberalization
- Détente (1970s): period of reduced tensions, arms control treaties (SALT I, SALT II)
- Soviet-Afghan War (1979-1989): Soviet invasion of Afghanistan, their "Vietnam"
- Fall of the Berlin Wall (November 9, 1989): symbol of Cold War's end
- Dissolution of the Soviet Union (December 25, 1991): Cold War officially ends

## Nuclear Arms Race

- 1945: US develops atomic bomb (Manhattan Project)
- 1949: Soviet Union tests its first atomic bomb
- 1952: US tests first hydrogen bomb (1,000× more powerful)
- 1953: Soviet Union tests hydrogen bomb
- Peak arsenals: US ~31,000 warheads (1967), USSR ~45,000 warheads (1986)
- MAD (Mutually Assured Destruction): neither side could attack without being destroyed in retaliation
- Arms control: INF Treaty (1987), START treaties reduced arsenals

## Legacy

- NATO and Warsaw Pact military alliances (Warsaw Pact dissolved 1991)
- Division of Germany (reunified 1990) and Korea (still divided)
- Nuclear proliferation concerns
- Space exploration advances (driven by competition)
- Proxy wars caused millions of deaths in developing countries
- Collapse of communism in Eastern Europe and the Soviet Union
