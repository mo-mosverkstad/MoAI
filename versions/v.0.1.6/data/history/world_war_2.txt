# World War II

## Overview

World War II (1939-1945) was the deadliest conflict in human history, involving more than 30 countries and resulting in an estimated 70-85 million deaths. The war pitted the Allies (primarily the United Kingdom, Soviet Union, United States, and China) against the Axis powers (Germany, Italy, and Japan).

## Causes

The war's origins include:

- The Treaty of Versailles (1919): imposed harsh reparations on Germany after World War I
- The Great Depression: economic collapse fueled extremist political movements
- Rise of fascism: Adolf Hitler in Germany, Benito Mussolini in Italy
- Japanese expansionism: invasion of Manchuria (1931) and China (1937)
- Failure of appeasement: the Munich Agreement (1938) failed to prevent German aggression

## Major Events

- September 1, 1939: Germany invades Poland; Britain and France declare war
- June 1940: France falls to Germany; the Battle of Britain begins
- June 22, 1941: Germany invades the Soviet Union (Operation Barbarossa)
- December 7, 1941: Japan attacks Pearl Harbor; the United States enters the war
- June 6, 1944: D-Day — Allied forces land in Normandy, France
- February 1945: Yalta Conference — Roosevelt, Churchill, and Stalin plan the postwar world
- May 8, 1945: V-E Day — Germany surrenders unconditionally
- August 6 and 9, 1945: Atomic bombs dropped on Hiroshima and Nagasaki
- September 2, 1945: V-J Day — Japan formally surrenders

## The Holocaust

The Holocaust was the systematic genocide of six million European Jews by Nazi Germany, along with millions of others including Roma, disabled people, political prisoners, and Soviet POWs. The Nazis established concentration and extermination camps including Auschwitz, Treblinka, and Sobibor.

## Consequences

- United Nations founded in 1945 to prevent future conflicts
- The Cold War began between the United States and Soviet Union
- Decolonization accelerated across Asia and Africa
- The Nuremberg Trials established precedents for international criminal law
- The Marshall Plan rebuilt Western Europe's economy
- Japan adopted a pacifist constitution and became a democracy
- The state of Israel was established in 1948
