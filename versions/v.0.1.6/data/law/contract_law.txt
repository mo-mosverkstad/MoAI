# Contract Law

## Definition

Contract law governs agreements between parties that create mutual legal obligations. A valid contract requires offer, acceptance, consideration (something of value exchanged), capacity (legal ability to contract), and legality (lawful purpose).

## Formation

- Offer: a clear proposal to enter into an agreement on specific terms
- Acceptance: unqualified agreement to the terms of the offer
- Consideration: each party must provide something of value (money, services, goods, promises)
- Intention: parties must intend to create legal relations
- Capacity: parties must be of legal age and sound mind

## Types of Contracts

- Express contracts: terms explicitly stated (written or oral)
- Implied contracts: terms inferred from conduct or circumstances
- Bilateral contracts: both parties make promises (most common)
- Unilateral contracts: one party makes a promise in exchange for an act
- Void contracts: have no legal effect from the beginning
- Voidable contracts: valid but can be cancelled by one party (e.g., due to misrepresentation)

## Breach of Contract

A breach occurs when a party fails to perform their contractual obligations:

- Material breach: a significant failure that defeats the purpose of the contract
- Minor breach: a partial failure that doesn't destroy the contract's value
- Anticipatory breach: one party indicates they will not perform before the deadline

## Remedies

- Damages: monetary compensation for losses caused by the breach
- Specific performance: court orders the breaching party to fulfill the contract
- Rescission: cancellation of the contract, returning parties to their pre-contract positions
- Injunction: court order preventing a party from doing something
