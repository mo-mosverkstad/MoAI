# Intellectual Property Law

## Definition

Intellectual property (IP) law protects creations of the mind — inventions, literary and artistic works, designs, symbols, names, and images used in commerce. IP rights give creators exclusive control over the use of their creations for a limited period.

## Patents

A patent grants the inventor exclusive rights to make, use, and sell an invention for a limited period (typically 20 years from filing).

Requirements for patentability:

- Novelty: the invention must be new
- Non-obviousness: it must not be obvious to someone skilled in the field
- Utility: it must have a practical application
- Disclosure: the inventor must describe the invention in sufficient detail for others to reproduce it

## Copyright

Copyright protects original works of authorship including literature, music, art, film, and software. It arises automatically upon creation — no registration required (though registration provides additional benefits).

- Duration: typically life of the author plus 70 years
- Rights protected: reproduction, distribution, public performance, adaptation
- Fair use: limited use for criticism, commentary, education, and parody without permission
- Public domain: works whose copyright has expired are free for anyone to use

## Trademarks

A trademark is a sign (word, logo, sound, color) that distinguishes goods or services of one business from another.

- Registration provides nationwide protection and legal presumption of ownership
- Duration: potentially indefinite, as long as the mark is used and renewed
- Infringement: using a confusingly similar mark on related goods or services
- Famous marks: receive broader protection against dilution (e.g., Coca-Cola, Apple)

## Trade Secrets

A trade secret is confidential business information that provides a competitive advantage (formulas, processes, customer lists). Unlike patents, trade secrets have no expiration but lose protection if disclosed. The Coca-Cola formula is the most famous trade secret.

## International IP Protection

- WIPO (World Intellectual Property Organization): administers international IP treaties
- TRIPS Agreement: WTO agreement setting minimum IP protection standards for member nations
- Paris Convention: international protection for patents and trademarks
- Berne Convention: international protection for copyright
