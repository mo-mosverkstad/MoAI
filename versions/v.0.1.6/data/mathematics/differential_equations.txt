# Differential Equations

## Definition

A differential equation is a mathematical equation that relates a function to its derivatives. Differential equations describe how quantities change over time or space and are fundamental to physics, engineering, biology, and economics.

## Types

- Ordinary differential equations (ODEs): involve functions of one variable and their derivatives (dy/dx = f(x,y))
- Partial differential equations (PDEs): involve functions of multiple variables and their partial derivatives (∂u/∂t = k∂²u/∂x²)
- Linear vs. nonlinear: linear equations have solutions that can be superposed; nonlinear equations are generally much harder to solve
- Order: determined by the highest derivative present (first-order, second-order, etc.)

## Famous Differential Equations

- Newton's second law: F = ma, or m(d²x/dt²) = F(x,t) — describes motion of objects
- Heat equation: ∂u/∂t = k∂²u/∂x² — describes how temperature distributes over time
- Wave equation: ∂²u/∂t² = c²∂²u/∂x² — describes propagation of waves (sound, light, water)
- Schrödinger equation: iℏ∂ψ/∂t = Ĥψ — fundamental equation of quantum mechanics
- Navier-Stokes equations: describe fluid flow (one of the Millennium Prize Problems — existence and smoothness of solutions is unproven)
- Maxwell's equations: describe electromagnetism (four coupled PDEs)
- Lotka-Volterra equations: model predator-prey population dynamics

## Solution Methods

- Separation of variables: rearrange so each variable is on one side
- Integrating factors: multiply by a function to make the equation exact
- Characteristic equation: convert linear ODEs to algebraic equations
- Laplace transform: convert differential equations to algebraic equations in a transformed domain
- Numerical methods: when analytical solutions don't exist (Euler's method, Runge-Kutta)
- Finite element method: approximate solutions to PDEs by dividing the domain into small elements

## Applications

- Physics: motion, heat transfer, electromagnetism, quantum mechanics, general relativity
- Engineering: circuit analysis, structural mechanics, control systems, fluid dynamics
- Biology: population growth, epidemiology (SIR model), neural activity
- Economics: economic growth models, option pricing (Black-Scholes equation)
- Chemistry: reaction kinetics, diffusion
- Climate science: atmospheric and ocean circulation models
