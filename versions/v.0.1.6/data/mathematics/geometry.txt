# Geometry

## Definition

Geometry is the branch of mathematics concerned with the properties and relations of points, lines, surfaces, and solids. The word comes from Greek: geo (earth) + metron (measurement). Euclid's Elements (300 BC) systematized geometry and remained the standard textbook for over 2,000 years.

## Euclidean Geometry

Based on five postulates (axioms), including the parallel postulate:

- Two points determine a unique line
- A line segment can be extended indefinitely
- A circle can be drawn with any center and radius
- All right angles are equal
- Parallel postulate: through a point not on a line, exactly one parallel line exists

## Fundamental Shapes

- Triangle: three sides, angles sum to 180°. Types: equilateral, isosceles, scalene, right.
- Pythagorean theorem: in a right triangle, a² + b² = c² (where c is the hypotenuse)
- Circle: all points equidistant from center. Area = πr², circumference = 2πr
- Quadrilaterals: four-sided polygons (square, rectangle, parallelogram, trapezoid, rhombus)
- Regular polygons: all sides and angles equal (pentagon, hexagon, octagon)

## Area and Volume Formulas

- Rectangle: A = length × width
- Triangle: A = ½ × base × height
- Circle: A = πr²
- Sphere: V = (4/3)πr³, surface area = 4πr²
- Cylinder: V = πr²h
- Cone: V = (1/3)πr²h

## Non-Euclidean Geometry

Geometries that reject the parallel postulate:

- Hyperbolic geometry (Lobachevsky, Bolyai, 1830s): through a point, infinitely many parallel lines exist. Space has negative curvature (saddle-shaped).
- Elliptic/spherical geometry (Riemann): no parallel lines exist (all great circles on a sphere intersect). Space has positive curvature.
- General relativity uses non-Euclidean geometry: massive objects curve spacetime.

## Topology

Topology studies properties preserved under continuous deformation (stretching, bending — not tearing or gluing):

- A coffee mug and a donut are topologically equivalent (both have one hole)
- Möbius strip: a surface with only one side and one edge
- Klein bottle: a closed surface with no inside or outside (cannot exist in 3D without self-intersection)
- Euler characteristic: V - E + F = 2 for any convex polyhedron (vertices - edges + faces)

## Applications

- Architecture and engineering: structural design, surveying
- Computer graphics: 3D modeling, rendering, animation
- Navigation: GPS, cartography, geodesy
- Physics: spacetime geometry, crystallography
- Art: perspective drawing, tessellations (M.C. Escher)
