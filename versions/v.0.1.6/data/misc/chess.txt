# Chess

## Overview

Chess is a two-player strategy board game played on an 8×8 grid. It is one of the world's oldest and most popular games, with an estimated 600 million regular players. Chess is often called "the game of kings" and has been studied extensively as a model of strategic thinking.

## History

- 6th century AD: originated in India as chaturanga (four divisions of the military)
- 7th century: spread to Persia (shatranj), then to the Arab world
- 10th century: reached Europe through Moorish Spain and trade routes
- 15th century: modern rules established in Spain (queen and bishop gained current powers)
- 1886: first official World Chess Championship (Wilhelm Steinitz)
- 1997: IBM's Deep Blue defeats world champion Garry Kasparov
- 2017: AlphaZero learns chess from scratch in 4 hours and defeats the strongest chess engine

## The Pieces

Each player starts with 16 pieces:

- King (1): moves one square in any direction; must be protected (checkmate loses the game)
- Queen (1): moves any number of squares in any direction (most powerful piece)
- Rook (2): moves any number of squares horizontally or vertically
- Bishop (2): moves any number of squares diagonally
- Knight (2): moves in an L-shape (2+1 squares), can jump over other pieces
- Pawn (8): moves forward one square (two on first move), captures diagonally

## Basic Strategy

- Control the center: pieces in the center control more squares
- Develop pieces: move pieces off the back rank early in the game
- King safety: castle early to protect the king
- Pawn structure: pawns cannot move backward; their structure shapes the game
- Material advantage: capturing more valuable pieces than you lose
- Tactics: forks, pins, skewers, discovered attacks, sacrifices

## World Champions

- Wilhelm Steinitz (1886-1894): first official champion, developed positional play
- José Raúl Capablanca (1921-1927): Cuban prodigy known for elegant endgame technique
- Bobby Fischer (1972-1975): American who defeated Soviet dominance during the Cold War
- Garry Kasparov (1985-2000): widely considered the greatest player of all time
- Magnus Carlsen (2013-2023): Norwegian prodigy, highest-rated player in history (2882)

## Chess and Computers

- 1950: Claude Shannon describes how a computer could play chess
- 1997: Deep Blue defeats Kasparov (brute-force search, 200 million positions per second)
- 2017: AlphaZero uses neural networks and self-play (no human knowledge)
- Modern engines (Stockfish, Leela Chess Zero) far exceed human ability
- Online chess: Chess.com and Lichess have over 100 million users combined
