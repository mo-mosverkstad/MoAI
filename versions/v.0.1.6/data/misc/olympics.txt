# The Olympic Games

## Ancient Olympics

The ancient Olympic Games were held in Olympia, Greece from 776 BC to 393 AD — a span of over 1,100 years. They were part of a religious festival honoring Zeus. Only free Greek men could compete. Events included running, wrestling, boxing, chariot racing, and the pentathlon. A sacred truce (ekecheiria) halted wars during the games.

## Modern Olympics

The modern Olympic Games were revived by Pierre de Coubertin in 1896 in Athens, Greece. The International Olympic Committee (IOC) governs the games.

- Summer Olympics: held every 4 years since 1896 (except during World Wars)
- Winter Olympics: held every 4 years since 1924, offset by 2 years from Summer Games since 1994
- Paralympic Games: for athletes with disabilities, held immediately after the Olympics since 1960
- Youth Olympic Games: for athletes aged 15-18, held since 2010

## Olympic Symbols

- Five rings: represent the five continents (blue, yellow, black, green, red on white background)
- Olympic flame: lit in Olympia, Greece and carried by relay to the host city
- Olympic motto: "Citius, Altius, Fortius — Communiter" (Faster, Higher, Stronger — Together)
- Olympic oath: athletes pledge fair competition

## Notable Host Cities

- Athens 1896: first modern Olympics, 14 nations, 241 athletes
- Berlin 1936: controversially hosted by Nazi Germany, Jesse Owens won 4 gold medals
- Tokyo 1964: first Olympics in Asia, introduced satellite broadcasting
- Munich 1972: terrorist attack killed 11 Israeli athletes
- Moscow 1980 / Los Angeles 1984: Cold War boycotts
- Beijing 2008: opening ceremony watched by 4 billion people
- Tokyo 2020: held in 2021 due to COVID-19 pandemic, first Olympics without spectators
- Paris 2024: events held at iconic landmarks (Eiffel Tower, Versailles)

## Sports

The Summer Olympics features over 30 sports and 300+ events. Core sports include athletics (track and field), swimming, gymnastics, cycling, and rowing. New sports are periodically added (skateboarding, surfing, and sport climbing were added in 2020).

## Records and Legends

- Most decorated Olympian: Michael Phelps (USA) — 28 medals (23 gold) in swimming
- Usain Bolt (Jamaica): fastest human, 100m in 9.58 seconds, 200m in 19.19 seconds
- Nadia Comăneci (Romania): first perfect 10 in gymnastics (1976)
- Carl Lewis (USA): 9 gold medals in athletics across 4 Olympics
