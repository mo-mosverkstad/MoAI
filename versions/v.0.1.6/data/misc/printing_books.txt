# Printing and Books

## History of Writing Materials

- Clay tablets (3400 BC): Sumerians pressed cuneiform into wet clay
- Papyrus (3000 BC): Egyptians made sheets from the papyrus plant
- Parchment (200 BC): animal skins prepared for writing, more durable than papyrus
- Paper (105 AD): invented in China by Cai Lun using bark, hemp, and rags
- Paper reached the Islamic world by the 8th century and Europe by the 12th century

## The Printing Press

Johannes Gutenberg invented the movable-type printing press in Mainz, Germany around 1440. This is considered one of the most important inventions in human history.

Key innovations:

- Movable metal type: individual letters cast in lead alloy, reusable
- Oil-based ink: adhered better to metal type than water-based inks
- Adapted wine/olive press: applied even pressure to paper

Impact:

- Gutenberg Bible (1455): first major book printed, approximately 180 copies
- Books became 80% cheaper within 50 years
- Literacy rates increased dramatically
- Enabled the Protestant Reformation (Luther's 95 Theses spread rapidly)
- Accelerated the Scientific Revolution by enabling rapid sharing of discoveries
- Standardized languages through printed grammars and dictionaries

## Modern Printing

- Lithography (1796): printing from a flat stone or metal plate
- Rotary press (1843): continuous printing on rolls of paper at high speed
- Offset printing (1875): image transferred to a rubber blanket then to paper
- Laser printing (1969): Xerox develops the first laser printer
- Inkjet printing (1976): drops of ink sprayed onto paper
- 3D printing (1984): additive manufacturing layer by layer

## The Book Industry

- ISBN system: International Standard Book Number identifies every published book
- Approximately 2.2 million new books published worldwide annually
- E-books: digital books readable on devices (Kindle launched 2007)
- Audiobooks: fastest-growing segment of the publishing industry
- Print-on-demand: books printed individually as ordered, reducing waste
- Self-publishing: authors bypass traditional publishers (Amazon KDP)

## Libraries

- Library of Alexandria (3rd century BC): largest library of the ancient world, estimated 400,000 scrolls
- Library of Congress (1800): largest library in the world, over 170 million items
- Public library movement: Andrew Carnegie funded 2,509 libraries worldwide (1883-1929)
- Digital libraries: Project Gutenberg (1971) offers over 70,000 free e-books
