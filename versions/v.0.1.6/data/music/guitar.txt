# The Guitar

## History

The guitar's ancestors include the lute, vihuela, and oud, dating back thousands of years. The modern six-string guitar emerged in Spain in the late 18th century. Antonio de Torres Jurado (1817-1892) established the design of the modern classical guitar around 1850.

## Types

- Classical guitar: nylon strings, wide neck, fingerpicked, used for classical and flamenco
- Acoustic guitar: steel strings, narrower neck, louder projection, used for folk, country, and pop
- Electric guitar: solid or semi-hollow body, magnetic pickups convert string vibration to electrical signal
- Bass guitar: four to six strings tuned an octave lower, provides rhythmic and harmonic foundation
- Twelve-string guitar: paired strings create a rich, shimmering sound

## How It Works

- Six strings tuned E-A-D-G-B-E (standard tuning)
- Frets divide the neck into semitone intervals
- Pressing a string against a fret shortens its vibrating length, raising the pitch
- The body amplifies vibrations (acoustic) or pickups detect them (electric)
- Electric guitars use amplifiers and effects pedals to shape their sound

## Influential Guitarists

- Andrés Segovia (1893-1987): established classical guitar as a concert instrument
- Robert Johnson (1911-1938): legendary blues guitarist, influenced all rock guitar
- Jimi Hendrix (1942-1970): revolutionized electric guitar with feedback, distortion, and wah-wah
- B.B. King (1925-2015): "King of the Blues," expressive vibrato and string bending
- Jimmy Page (born 1944): Led Zeppelin guitarist, pioneered hard rock guitar
- Eddie Van Halen (1955-2020): introduced two-handed tapping technique to rock

## Guitar Effects

Electric guitarists use effects to modify their sound:

- Distortion/overdrive: adds grit and sustain (the sound of rock)
- Reverb: simulates acoustic spaces
- Delay/echo: repeats the signal after a time interval
- Wah-wah: sweeps a frequency peak up and down
- Chorus: thickens the sound by adding slightly detuned copies
- Looper: records and plays back phrases for layering
