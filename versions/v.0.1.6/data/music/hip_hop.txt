# Hip Hop

## Origins

Hip hop originated in the Bronx, New York City in the early 1970s, created primarily by African American and Latino youth. DJ Kool Herc is credited with pioneering the genre by isolating and extending drum breaks from funk and soul records at block parties in 1973.

## Four Elements

Hip hop culture traditionally comprises four elements:

- MCing (rapping): rhythmic vocal delivery over beats
- DJing: turntablism, scratching, beat juggling, and mixing
- Breaking (breakdancing): athletic dance style performed to hip hop music
- Graffiti art: visual art form using spray paint on public surfaces

## Musical Characteristics

- Sampling: incorporating portions of existing recordings into new compositions
- Beatmaking: creating instrumental tracks using drum machines, samplers, and DAWs
- Flow: the rhythm and rhyme patterns of rap delivery
- Lyricism: wordplay, metaphor, storytelling, and social commentary
- 808 bass: deep sub-bass from the Roland TR-808 drum machine

## Evolution

- Old school (1973-1984): party music, simple rhymes (Sugarhill Gang, Grandmaster Flash)
- Golden age (1986-1994): diverse styles, lyrical complexity (Run-DMC, Public Enemy, A Tribe Called Quest, Nas)
- Gangsta rap (late 1980s-1990s): street life narratives (N.W.A, Tupac, Notorious B.I.G.)
- East Coast vs. West Coast (1990s): rivalry between New York and Los Angeles scenes
- Southern hip hop (2000s): crunk, trap, bounce (OutKast, T.I., Lil Wayne)
- Trap (2010s-present): hi-hats, 808 bass, dark melodies (Future, Migos, Travis Scott)

## Global Impact

Hip hop is now the most consumed music genre globally. It has influenced fashion, language, politics, and visual art worldwide. Artists like Jay-Z, Kanye West, Kendrick Lamar, and Drake have become cultural icons transcending music.
