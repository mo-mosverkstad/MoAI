# Music Theory

## Definition

Music theory is the study of the practices and possibilities of music. It examines the fundamental elements that compose musical works and provides a framework for understanding, analyzing, and creating music.

## Pitch and Scales

- Pitch: the perceived frequency of a sound (higher frequency = higher pitch)
- Octave: the interval between one pitch and another with double its frequency
- Chromatic scale: all 12 semitones within an octave
- Major scale: a seven-note scale with a specific pattern of whole and half steps (W-W-H-W-W-W-H)
- Minor scale: a seven-note scale with a darker sound (W-H-W-W-H-W-W for natural minor)
- Pentatonic scale: a five-note scale common in folk music worldwide

## Intervals

An interval is the distance between two pitches:

- Unison: same pitch
- Minor/Major second: 1-2 semitones
- Minor/Major third: 3-4 semitones
- Perfect fourth: 5 semitones
- Perfect fifth: 7 semitones
- Octave: 12 semitones

## Chords and Harmony

A chord is three or more notes sounded simultaneously:

- Major triad: root + major third + perfect fifth (sounds bright, happy)
- Minor triad: root + minor third + perfect fifth (sounds dark, sad)
- Diminished triad: root + minor third + diminished fifth (sounds tense)
- Seventh chords: triads with an added seventh (dominant 7th, major 7th, minor 7th)

## Rhythm and Meter

- Beat: the basic unit of time in music
- Tempo: the speed of the beat (measured in BPM — beats per minute)
- Meter: the grouping of beats (4/4 = four beats per measure, 3/4 = waltz time)
- Syncopation: emphasis on normally weak beats
- Polyrhythm: two or more conflicting rhythms played simultaneously

## Key Signatures

A key signature indicates which notes are sharp or flat throughout a piece:

- C major / A minor: no sharps or flats
- G major / E minor: one sharp (F#)
- F major / D minor: one flat (Bb)
- Key relationships: the circle of fifths shows how keys relate to each other
