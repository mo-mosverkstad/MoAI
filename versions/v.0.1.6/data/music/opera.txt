# Opera

## Definition

Opera is a form of theater in which music is the primary artistic element, combining singing, orchestral music, acting, and often dance. The text (libretto) is sung rather than spoken, with the orchestra providing accompaniment and dramatic commentary.

## History

- 1597: Dafne by Jacopo Peri — considered the first opera (music lost)
- 1607: L'Orfeo by Claudio Monteverdi — earliest opera still regularly performed
- 1700s: Opera seria (serious) and opera buffa (comic) develop as distinct genres
- 1786: Mozart's The Marriage of Figaro revolutionizes comic opera
- 1800s: Romantic opera reaches its peak with Verdi and Wagner
- 1900s: Puccini, Strauss, and modern composers expand the form

## Voice Types

Opera singers are classified by vocal range and quality:

- Soprano: highest female voice (Queen of the Night in The Magic Flute)
- Mezzo-soprano: middle female voice (Carmen)
- Contralto: lowest female voice (rare, Erda in Wagner's Ring)
- Tenor: highest male voice (the romantic hero — Rodolfo in La Bohème)
- Baritone: middle male voice (Figaro, Rigoletto)
- Bass: lowest male voice (villains and authority figures — Sarastro, Boris Godunov)

## Famous Operas

- The Magic Flute (Mozart, 1791): fairy-tale singspiel about love and enlightenment
- La Traviata (Verdi, 1853): tragic love story of a Parisian courtesan
- Carmen (Bizet, 1875): passionate tale of love and jealousy in Seville
- La Bohème (Puccini, 1896): young artists struggling with love and poverty in Paris
- Der Ring des Nibelungen (Wagner, 1876): four-opera cycle lasting 15 hours, based on Norse mythology
- Madama Butterfly (Puccini, 1904): tragic story of a Japanese woman abandoned by an American officer

## Famous Opera Houses

- La Scala, Milan (1778): Italy's most prestigious opera house
- Vienna State Opera (1869): one of the world's leading opera companies
- Metropolitan Opera, New York (1883): largest classical music organization in North America
- Royal Opera House, London (1732): home of The Royal Opera and The Royal Ballet
- Sydney Opera House (1973): iconic building, UNESCO World Heritage Site
