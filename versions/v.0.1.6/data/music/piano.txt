# The Piano

## History

The piano was invented by Bartolomeo Cristofori in Florence, Italy, around 1700. He called it "gravicembalo col piano e forte" (harpsichord with soft and loud), later shortened to pianoforte and then piano. Unlike the harpsichord, the piano could vary its volume based on how hard the keys were struck.

## How It Works

The piano is a keyboard instrument that produces sound through hammers striking strings:

- 88 keys (52 white, 36 black) spanning over 7 octaves
- Each key activates a felt-covered hammer that strikes one to three strings
- Strings vibrate and transmit sound through a wooden soundboard
- Dampers stop the strings from vibrating when keys are released
- Sustain pedal lifts all dampers, allowing strings to ring freely
- Soft pedal (una corda) shifts hammers to strike fewer strings

## Types of Pianos

- Grand piano: horizontal strings, superior tone and action (concert grands are 2.7 meters long)
- Upright piano: vertical strings, more compact for homes and studios
- Digital piano: electronic instrument that samples or models acoustic piano sound
- Electric piano: electromechanical instrument (Fender Rhodes, Wurlitzer)

## Famous Pianists

- Frédéric Chopin (1810-1849): Polish composer who wrote almost exclusively for piano
- Franz Liszt (1811-1886): Hungarian virtuoso who expanded piano technique to its limits
- Sergei Rachmaninoff (1873-1943): Russian composer-pianist with legendary technique and large hands
- Glenn Gould (1932-1982): Canadian pianist famous for his Bach interpretations
- Vladimir Horowitz (1903-1989): considered one of the greatest pianists of the 20th century
- Martha Argerich (born 1941): Argentine pianist renowned for her passionate performances

## The Piano in Different Genres

- Classical: solo recitals, concertos, chamber music, art song accompaniment
- Jazz: stride piano, bebop, Bill Evans' impressionistic style, Thelonious Monk
- Blues and boogie-woogie: left-hand bass patterns, right-hand improvisation
- Rock and pop: Elton John, Billy Joel, Stevie Wonder
- Ragtime: syncopated piano music, Scott Joplin's "The Entertainer"
