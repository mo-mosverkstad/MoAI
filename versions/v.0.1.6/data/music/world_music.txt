# World Music Traditions

## Indian Classical Music

Indian classical music is one of the oldest musical traditions, dating back over 3,000 years. It has two main traditions:

- Hindustani (North India): influenced by Persian music, uses ragas and talas. Instruments include sitar, tabla, sarangi, and tanpura. Ravi Shankar popularized it internationally.
- Carnatic (South India): more structured and composition-based. Instruments include veena, mridangam, and violin. Based on a system of 72 parent scales (melakarta ragas).

Key concepts: raga (melodic framework), tala (rhythmic cycle), drone (sustained reference pitch), improvisation within strict rules.

## African Music

African music is extraordinarily diverse across the continent:

- West African drumming: djembe, talking drums, complex polyrhythms
- Highlife (Ghana/Nigeria): guitar-based dance music blending African and Western elements
- Afrobeat (Nigeria): created by Fela Kuti, combining jazz, funk, and Yoruba music
- Mbira music (Zimbabwe): thumb piano creating interlocking melodic patterns
- South African music: mbaqanga, kwaito, and choral traditions (Ladysmith Black Mambazo)

## Latin American Music

- Samba (Brazil): syncopated rhythms, percussion-driven, central to Carnival
- Bossa nova (Brazil): gentle fusion of samba and jazz (João Gilberto, Antonio Carlos Jobim)
- Tango (Argentina): passionate dance music with bandoneón (accordion-like instrument)
- Salsa (Cuba/Puerto Rico/New York): energetic dance music combining son cubano with jazz
- Mariachi (Mexico): ensemble of violins, trumpets, and guitars

## East Asian Music

- Chinese classical: pentatonic scales, instruments include erhu, pipa, guzheng, and dizi
- Japanese traditional: gagaku (court music), shakuhachi (bamboo flute), koto (zither), taiko drums
- Korean: pansori (dramatic singing), gayageum (zither), samulnori (percussion ensemble)
- Gamelan (Indonesia): ensemble of metallophones, gongs, and drums with interlocking patterns

## Celtic Music

Traditional music of Ireland, Scotland, Wales, and Brittany:

- Instruments: fiddle, tin whistle, uilleann pipes, bodhrán, harp, accordion
- Forms: jigs (6/8 time), reels (4/4 time), hornpipes, slow airs
- Sean-nós: unaccompanied traditional Irish singing
- Revival: The Chieftains, Clannad, Enya brought Celtic music to global audiences
