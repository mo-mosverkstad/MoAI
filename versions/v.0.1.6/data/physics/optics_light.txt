# Optics and Light

## What is Light

Light is electromagnetic radiation visible to the human eye (wavelengths approximately 380-700 nanometers). It exhibits both wave and particle properties (wave-particle duality). Light travels at 299,792,458 m/s in vacuum — the fastest speed possible for any information or matter.

## The Electromagnetic Spectrum

From longest to shortest wavelength:

- Radio waves: communication, broadcasting (wavelengths: meters to kilometers)
- Microwaves: cooking, radar, Wi-Fi (millimeters to centimeters)
- Infrared: heat radiation, remote controls, thermal imaging (700 nm - 1 mm)
- Visible light: the only portion humans can see (380-700 nm)
- Ultraviolet: causes sunburn, used for sterilization (10-380 nm)
- X-rays: medical imaging, security scanning (0.01-10 nm)
- Gamma rays: nuclear reactions, cancer treatment (< 0.01 nm)

All electromagnetic radiation travels at the speed of light and differs only in wavelength/frequency.

## Wave Properties of Light

- Reflection: light bounces off surfaces (angle of incidence = angle of reflection)
- Refraction: light bends when passing between media of different density (explains why a straw looks bent in water)
- Diffraction: light bends around obstacles and through narrow openings
- Interference: waves can reinforce (constructive) or cancel (destructive) each other
- Polarization: light waves can be filtered to vibrate in only one plane

## Color

- White light contains all visible wavelengths (demonstrated by Newton's prism experiment, 1666)
- Red: ~700 nm, Orange: ~600 nm, Yellow: ~580 nm, Green: ~530 nm, Blue: ~470 nm, Violet: ~400 nm
- Objects appear colored because they absorb some wavelengths and reflect others
- Additive color mixing (light): red + green + blue = white (RGB displays)
- Subtractive color mixing (pigments): cyan + magenta + yellow = black (CMYK printing)

## Lenses and Mirrors

- Convex lens: converges light to a focal point (magnifying glass, camera, eye)
- Concave lens: diverges light (corrects nearsightedness)
- Concave mirror: converges light (telescopes, satellite dishes, headlights)
- Convex mirror: diverges light, provides wide field of view (car side mirrors)
- Focal length: distance from lens/mirror to focal point

## Lasers

LASER = Light Amplification by Stimulated Emission of Radiation:

- Produces coherent, monochromatic, highly directional light
- Invented in 1960 by Theodore Maiman (ruby laser)
- Applications: surgery, fiber optic communication, barcode scanners, laser cutting, CD/DVD/Blu-ray, LIDAR, laser pointers, holography
- Types: gas lasers (HeNe), solid-state (Nd:YAG), semiconductor (diode lasers), fiber lasers
