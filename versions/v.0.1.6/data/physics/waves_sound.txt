# Waves and Sound

## What is a Wave

A wave is a disturbance that transfers energy from one place to another without transferring matter. The medium (air, water, string) oscillates but doesn't travel with the wave.

## Types of Waves

- Mechanical waves: require a medium (sound, water waves, seismic waves)
- Electromagnetic waves: don't require a medium (light, radio, X-rays)
- Transverse waves: oscillation perpendicular to direction of travel (light, waves on a string)
- Longitudinal waves: oscillation parallel to direction of travel (sound, compression waves)

## Wave Properties

- Wavelength (λ): distance between consecutive crests or compressions
- Frequency (f): number of complete oscillations per second (measured in Hertz, Hz)
- Amplitude: maximum displacement from equilibrium (determines loudness for sound, brightness for light)
- Speed: v = fλ (speed equals frequency times wavelength)
- Period: time for one complete oscillation (T = 1/f)

## Sound

Sound is a longitudinal mechanical wave — compressions and rarefactions of air molecules:

- Speed of sound in air: approximately 343 m/s at 20°C (varies with temperature and medium)
- Speed in water: ~1,480 m/s; in steel: ~5,960 m/s
- Human hearing range: 20 Hz to 20,000 Hz
- Infrasound: below 20 Hz (elephants communicate at 14-35 Hz)
- Ultrasound: above 20,000 Hz (medical imaging, bat echolocation, dog whistles)

## Sound Intensity and Decibels

Sound intensity is measured in decibels (dB), a logarithmic scale:

- 0 dB: threshold of hearing
- 30 dB: whisper
- 60 dB: normal conversation
- 85 dB: heavy traffic (prolonged exposure causes hearing damage)
- 100 dB: motorcycle
- 120 dB: threshold of pain
- 140 dB: jet engine at 30 meters

Each 10 dB increase represents a 10-fold increase in intensity.

## Wave Phenomena

- Reflection: waves bounce off surfaces (echo)
- Refraction: waves change direction when entering a different medium
- Diffraction: waves bend around obstacles or through openings
- Interference: waves combine constructively (louder) or destructively (quieter)
- Resonance: a system vibrates with maximum amplitude at its natural frequency
- Doppler effect: frequency changes when source or observer moves (ambulance siren pitch change, redshift of distant galaxies)

## Applications

- Musical instruments: vibrating strings (guitar), air columns (flute), membranes (drums)
- Sonar: using sound echoes to detect objects underwater
- Ultrasound imaging: high-frequency sound waves create images of internal organs
- Noise cancellation: destructive interference eliminates unwanted sound
- Seismology: analyzing seismic waves reveals Earth's internal structure
