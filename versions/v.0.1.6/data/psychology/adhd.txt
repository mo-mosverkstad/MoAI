# Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD)

## Definition

ADHD is a neurodevelopmental disorder characterized by persistent patterns of inattention, hyperactivity, and impulsivity that interfere with functioning and development. It affects approximately 5-7% of children and 2-5% of adults worldwide.

## Presentations

- Predominantly inattentive: difficulty sustaining attention, easily distracted, forgetful, disorganized
- Predominantly hyperactive-impulsive: fidgeting, difficulty sitting still, excessive talking, interrupting, acting without thinking
- Combined presentation: symptoms of both inattention and hyperactivity-impulsivity

## Symptoms of Inattention

- Difficulty sustaining attention in tasks or play
- Frequently losing things necessary for tasks
- Easily distracted by extraneous stimuli
- Forgetful in daily activities
- Difficulty organizing tasks and managing time
- Avoidance of tasks requiring sustained mental effort
- Failure to follow through on instructions or finish tasks
- Appearing not to listen when spoken to directly

## Symptoms of Hyperactivity-Impulsivity

- Fidgeting, squirming, tapping hands or feet
- Leaving seat when remaining seated is expected
- Running or climbing in inappropriate situations
- Inability to engage in leisure activities quietly
- Talking excessively
- Blurting out answers before questions are completed
- Difficulty waiting one's turn
- Interrupting or intruding on others

## Neurobiology

- Prefrontal cortex: underactive, impairing executive functions
- Dopamine and norepinephrine: deficient signaling in frontal-striatal circuits
- Brain development: cortical maturation delayed by approximately 3 years
- Brain structure: slightly smaller prefrontal cortex, basal ganglia, and cerebellum
- Heritability: approximately 75% (one of the most heritable psychiatric conditions)

## Treatment

- Stimulant medications: methylphenidate (Ritalin) and amphetamines (Adderall) — effective in 70-80% of cases
- Non-stimulant medications: atomoxetine, guanfacine
- Behavioral therapy: organizational skills training, parent training, classroom accommodations
- Cognitive-behavioral therapy: addressing negative thought patterns and developing coping strategies
- Exercise: regular physical activity improves attention and executive function
- Environmental modifications: reducing distractions, breaking tasks into smaller steps, using timers and reminders

## ADHD in Adults

- Hyperactivity often decreases with age; inattention and impulsivity persist
- Associated with difficulties in work performance, relationships, and time management
- Higher rates of substance abuse, accidents, and comorbid conditions
- Often undiagnosed until adulthood, especially in women (who more often present with inattentive type)
