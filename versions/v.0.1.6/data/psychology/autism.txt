# Autism Spectrum Disorder

## Definition

Autism spectrum disorder (ASD) is a neurodevelopmental condition characterized by differences in social communication, restricted interests, and repetitive behaviors. It is called a "spectrum" because it varies widely in severity and presentation. Approximately 1 in 36 children in the United States is diagnosed with ASD (CDC, 2023).

## Core Characteristics

Social communication differences:

- Difficulty reading social cues (facial expressions, body language, tone of voice)
- Challenges with reciprocal conversation (turn-taking, staying on topic)
- Reduced eye contact or atypical use of gestures
- Difficulty understanding sarcasm, irony, or figurative language
- Challenges forming and maintaining friendships

Restricted and repetitive behaviors:

- Intense, focused interests in specific topics
- Repetitive movements (hand-flapping, rocking, spinning)
- Insistence on sameness and routines; distress at unexpected changes
- Sensory sensitivities (hypersensitivity or hyposensitivity to sounds, textures, lights)

## Diagnosis

- Typically diagnosed in early childhood (ages 2-4), though some are diagnosed later
- No medical test exists; diagnosis is based on behavioral observation and developmental history
- DSM-5 (2013) merged previous categories (autistic disorder, Asperger's syndrome, PDD-NOS) into one spectrum
- Severity levels: Level 1 (requiring support), Level 2 (requiring substantial support), Level 3 (requiring very substantial support)

## Causes

- Strongly genetic: heritability estimated at 80-90%
- Hundreds of genes contribute, each with small effect
- No single cause identified; likely involves gene-environment interactions
- Advanced parental age is a risk factor
- Vaccines do NOT cause autism — this claim has been thoroughly debunked by extensive research

## Strengths

Many autistic individuals have notable strengths:

- Exceptional attention to detail and pattern recognition
- Strong memory for facts and systems
- Honesty and directness in communication
- Deep expertise in areas of special interest
- Logical and systematic thinking
- Loyalty and consistency in relationships

## Support and Intervention

- Early intervention: speech therapy, occupational therapy, social skills training
- Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA): structured teaching of skills (controversial within autistic community)
- Speech-language therapy: improving communication abilities
- Sensory integration therapy: managing sensory sensitivities
- Accommodations: structured environments, visual schedules, noise-reducing headphones
- Neurodiversity movement: advocates acceptance of autism as natural variation rather than disorder to be cured
