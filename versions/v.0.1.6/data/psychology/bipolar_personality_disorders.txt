# Bipolar Disorder and Personality Disorders

## Bipolar Disorder

Bipolar disorder (formerly manic-depressive illness) is characterized by extreme mood swings between mania (or hypomania) and depression.

Types:

- Bipolar I: at least one manic episode (may include depressive episodes)
- Bipolar II: at least one hypomanic episode and one major depressive episode (no full mania)
- Cyclothymic disorder: chronic fluctuating mood with hypomanic and depressive symptoms that don't meet full criteria

Manic episode symptoms:

- Abnormally elevated, expansive, or irritable mood lasting at least 7 days
- Decreased need for sleep (feeling rested after 3 hours)
- Pressured speech, racing thoughts
- Grandiosity or inflated self-esteem
- Increased goal-directed activity or psychomotor agitation
- Risky behavior (spending sprees, sexual indiscretions, reckless driving)
- Possible psychotic features (delusions, hallucinations)

Treatment: mood stabilizers (lithium, valproate), atypical antipsychotics, psychoeducation, cognitive-behavioral therapy. Lifelong management typically required.

Prevalence: approximately 2.4% of the global population. Equal rates in men and women.

## Borderline Personality Disorder (BPD)

A pattern of instability in interpersonal relationships, self-image, emotions, and marked impulsivity:

- Frantic efforts to avoid real or imagined abandonment
- Unstable and intense relationships (idealization and devaluation)
- Identity disturbance: unstable self-image
- Impulsivity (spending, substance abuse, reckless driving, binge eating)
- Recurrent suicidal behavior or self-harm
- Emotional instability: intense episodes of dysphoria, irritability, or anxiety
- Chronic feelings of emptiness
- Inappropriate intense anger
- Transient paranoid ideation or dissociation under stress

Treatment: Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT, developed by Marsha Linehan) is the gold standard — teaches mindfulness, distress tolerance, emotion regulation, and interpersonal effectiveness.

## Narcissistic Personality Disorder

A pattern of grandiosity, need for admiration, and lack of empathy:

- Grandiose sense of self-importance
- Preoccupation with fantasies of unlimited success, power, or beauty
- Belief in being special and unique
- Requires excessive admiration
- Sense of entitlement
- Exploitative in relationships
- Lacks empathy
- Envious of others or believes others are envious
- Arrogant behaviors or attitudes

Affects approximately 1-6% of the population, more common in males.

## Antisocial Personality Disorder

A pattern of disregard for and violation of others' rights since age 15:

- Failure to conform to social norms (repeated illegal behavior)
- Deceitfulness (lying, using aliases, conning others)
- Impulsivity and failure to plan ahead
- Irritability and aggressiveness (repeated fights or assaults)
- Reckless disregard for safety of self or others
- Consistent irresponsibility
- Lack of remorse

Related to psychopathy (Hare's Psychopathy Checklist): superficial charm, grandiosity, pathological lying, manipulation, shallow affect, callousness. Affects approximately 3% of males and 1% of females.
