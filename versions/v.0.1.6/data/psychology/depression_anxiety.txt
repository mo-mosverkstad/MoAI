# Depression and Anxiety Disorders

## Major Depressive Disorder

Major depression is more than ordinary sadness — it is a persistent condition that affects how a person thinks, feels, and functions. It is the leading cause of disability worldwide, affecting approximately 280 million people.

Symptoms (at least 5 for 2+ weeks):

- Persistent sad, empty, or hopeless mood
- Loss of interest or pleasure in activities (anhedonia)
- Significant weight change or appetite disturbance
- Insomnia or hypersomnia
- Psychomotor agitation or retardation
- Fatigue or loss of energy
- Feelings of worthlessness or excessive guilt
- Difficulty concentrating or making decisions
- Recurrent thoughts of death or suicide

Causes: combination of genetic vulnerability (40% heritability), neurochemical imbalances (serotonin, norepinephrine, dopamine), stressful life events, and cognitive patterns (negative thinking styles).

Treatment: antidepressant medications (SSRIs, SNRIs), cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT), interpersonal therapy, exercise, electroconvulsive therapy (ECT) for severe cases.

## Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)

Excessive, uncontrollable worry about multiple areas of life for at least 6 months:

- Restlessness or feeling on edge
- Easy fatigue
- Difficulty concentrating
- Irritability
- Muscle tension
- Sleep disturbance

Affects approximately 3-5% of the population. More common in women.

## Panic Disorder

Recurrent unexpected panic attacks — sudden surges of intense fear with physical symptoms:

- Racing heart, chest pain
- Shortness of breath, choking sensation
- Dizziness, numbness, tingling
- Fear of dying or losing control
- Derealization (feeling of unreality)

Attacks peak within minutes. Fear of future attacks leads to avoidance behavior (agoraphobia).

## Social Anxiety Disorder

Intense fear of social situations where one might be judged, embarrassed, or humiliated:

- Fear of speaking in public, meeting new people, eating in front of others
- Physical symptoms: blushing, sweating, trembling, nausea
- Avoidance of feared situations
- Affects approximately 7% of the population
- Often begins in adolescence

## Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD)

Characterized by intrusive, unwanted thoughts (obsessions) and repetitive behaviors (compulsions):

- Common obsessions: contamination, harm, symmetry, forbidden thoughts
- Common compulsions: washing, checking, counting, ordering, mental rituals
- Compulsions temporarily reduce anxiety but reinforce the cycle
- Affects approximately 2-3% of the population
- Treatment: Exposure and Response Prevention (ERP), SSRIs
