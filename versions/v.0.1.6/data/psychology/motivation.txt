# Motivation

## Definition

Motivation is the process that initiates, guides, and sustains goal-directed behavior. It involves the biological, emotional, social, and cognitive forces that activate behavior and determine its direction, intensity, and persistence.

## Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs

Abraham Maslow (1943) proposed that human needs are arranged in a hierarchy:

1. Physiological needs: food, water, sleep, shelter (most basic)
2. Safety needs: security, stability, freedom from fear
3. Belongingness and love: friendship, intimacy, family, social connection
4. Esteem needs: achievement, recognition, respect, self-confidence
5. Self-actualization: realizing one's full potential, creativity, personal growth (highest)

Lower needs must be substantially satisfied before higher needs become motivating. However, the hierarchy is not rigid — people can pursue higher needs while lower ones are partially unmet.

## Intrinsic vs. Extrinsic Motivation

- Intrinsic motivation: doing something for its own sake (enjoyment, curiosity, challenge)
- Extrinsic motivation: doing something for external rewards or to avoid punishment (money, grades, praise)

Self-Determination Theory (Deci & Ryan) identifies three basic psychological needs:

- Autonomy: feeling in control of one's actions
- Competence: feeling effective and capable
- Relatedness: feeling connected to others

## Drive Theory

Drives are internal states of tension that motivate behavior to restore homeostasis:

- Hunger drive: low blood sugar triggers eating behavior
- Thirst drive: dehydration triggers drinking behavior
- Sex drive: hormonal and psychological factors motivate sexual behavior
- Drive reduction: satisfying a drive reduces tension and reinforces the behavior

## Achievement Motivation

- Need for achievement (McClelland): desire to accomplish difficult tasks and meet high standards
- Growth mindset (Dweck): believing abilities can be developed through effort leads to greater achievement
- Fixed mindset: believing abilities are innate leads to avoidance of challenges
- Flow (Csikszentmihalyi): state of complete absorption in an optimally challenging activity

## Procrastination

Procrastination is the voluntary delay of intended actions despite expecting negative consequences:

- Affects approximately 20% of adults chronically
- Related to poor emotion regulation rather than laziness
- Temporal discounting: immediate comfort is valued over future rewards
- Strategies: breaking tasks into small steps, implementation intentions, removing distractions
