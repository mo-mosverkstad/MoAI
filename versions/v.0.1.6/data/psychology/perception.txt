# Perception

## Definition

Perception is the process by which the brain organizes and interprets sensory information to give it meaning. It is not a passive recording of reality but an active construction — the brain fills in gaps, makes assumptions, and sometimes creates illusions.

## Visual Perception

The visual system processes light entering the eyes:

- Retina: contains photoreceptors (rods for dim light, cones for color)
- Optic nerve: transmits signals to the brain
- Visual cortex: processes features (edges, motion, color, faces)
- Ventral stream ("what" pathway): object recognition
- Dorsal stream ("where" pathway): spatial location and movement

## Gestalt Principles

The Gestalt psychologists identified rules the brain uses to organize visual information:

- Proximity: objects near each other are grouped together
- Similarity: similar objects are grouped together
- Closure: the brain completes incomplete figures
- Continuity: we perceive smooth, continuous patterns rather than abrupt changes
- Figure-ground: we separate objects (figure) from their background (ground)
- Common fate: objects moving in the same direction are grouped together

## Depth Perception

The brain uses multiple cues to perceive three-dimensional space:

- Binocular cues: require both eyes (retinal disparity, convergence)
- Monocular cues: work with one eye (linear perspective, texture gradient, relative size, overlap, shadow, motion parallax)

## Optical Illusions

Illusions reveal how the brain constructs perception:

- Müller-Lyer illusion: lines of equal length appear different due to arrow-like endings
- Ponzo illusion: identical lines appear different sizes due to converging lines (railroad tracks)
- Necker cube: ambiguous figure that flips between two interpretations
- Rubin's vase: figure-ground reversal (vase or two faces)
- Change blindness: failure to notice large changes in a visual scene

## Other Senses

- Auditory perception: sound localization, speech perception, music perception
- Haptic perception: touch, pressure, temperature, pain
- Olfaction: smell — directly connected to memory and emotion (limbic system)
- Gustation: taste — five basic tastes (sweet, sour, salty, bitter, umami)
- Proprioception: awareness of body position without looking
