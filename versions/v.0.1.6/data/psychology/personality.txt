# Personality

## Definition

Personality refers to the characteristic patterns of thoughts, feelings, and behaviors that make a person unique and relatively consistent across situations and over time. Personality psychology seeks to understand individual differences and predict behavior.

## The Big Five (OCEAN)

The most widely accepted model of personality traits:

- Openness to experience: curiosity, creativity, preference for novelty vs. routine
- Conscientiousness: organization, self-discipline, goal-directed behavior vs. carelessness
- Extraversion: sociability, assertiveness, positive emotions vs. introversion
- Agreeableness: cooperation, trust, empathy vs. antagonism
- Neuroticism: emotional instability, anxiety, moodiness vs. emotional stability

These traits are normally distributed in the population, relatively stable after age 30, and approximately 50% heritable.

## Psychoanalytic Theory

Sigmund Freud (1856-1939) proposed that personality is shaped by unconscious conflicts:

- Id: primitive drives seeking immediate gratification (pleasure principle)
- Ego: rational mediator between id and reality (reality principle)
- Superego: internalized moral standards (conscience)
- Defense mechanisms: unconscious strategies to reduce anxiety (repression, projection, denial, sublimation)
- Psychosexual stages: oral, anal, phallic, latency, genital

## Humanistic Theories

- Carl Rogers: people have an innate drive toward self-actualization. Healthy development requires unconditional positive regard.
- Abraham Maslow: self-actualized people are creative, spontaneous, accepting, and have peak experiences.

## Personality Assessment

- Self-report questionnaires: NEO-PI-R (Big Five), MMPI (clinical), Myers-Briggs (popular but less scientific)
- Projective tests: Rorschach inkblots, Thematic Apperception Test (TAT)
- Behavioral observation: systematic recording of behavior in natural or controlled settings
- Informant reports: ratings by people who know the individual well

## Nature vs. Nurture

Twin studies show that personality traits are approximately:

- 50% genetic (identical twins raised apart show similar personalities)
- 50% environmental (but mostly non-shared environment — unique experiences, not family)
- Shared family environment has surprisingly little effect on adult personality
