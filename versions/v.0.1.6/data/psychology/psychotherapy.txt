# Psychotherapy Approaches

## Definition

Psychotherapy (talk therapy) is the treatment of mental health conditions through psychological methods. A trained therapist helps individuals understand their thoughts, feelings, and behaviors, and develop strategies for change. Research shows psychotherapy is effective for most mental health conditions.

## Cognitive-Behavioral Therapy (CBT)

The most researched and widely practiced form of psychotherapy:

- Core principle: thoughts, feelings, and behaviors are interconnected; changing maladaptive thoughts changes emotions and behavior
- Cognitive restructuring: identifying and challenging distorted thinking patterns
- Behavioral experiments: testing beliefs through real-world actions
- Exposure therapy: gradually confronting feared situations
- Typically short-term (12-20 sessions), structured, and goal-oriented
- Strong evidence for depression, anxiety, PTSD, OCD, eating disorders, insomnia

## Psychodynamic Therapy

Based on psychoanalytic principles but more focused and shorter-term:

- Explores unconscious patterns and past experiences that influence current behavior
- Examines defense mechanisms and transference (projecting feelings onto the therapist)
- Focuses on insight — understanding the roots of emotional suffering
- Free association: saying whatever comes to mind without censoring
- Effective for depression, anxiety, personality disorders, and relationship difficulties

## Humanistic/Person-Centered Therapy

Developed by Carl Rogers:

- Core conditions: unconditional positive regard, empathy, and genuineness
- Non-directive: therapist follows the client's lead rather than imposing structure
- Focus on present experience and personal growth
- Belief that clients have innate capacity for self-healing given the right conditions
- Effective for depression, relationship issues, and personal development

## Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT)

Developed by Marsha Linehan for borderline personality disorder:

- Combines CBT with mindfulness and acceptance strategies
- Four skill modules: mindfulness, distress tolerance, emotion regulation, interpersonal effectiveness
- Includes individual therapy, group skills training, phone coaching, and therapist consultation team
- Effective for BPD, self-harm, suicidal behavior, substance abuse, eating disorders

## Family and Couples Therapy

- Systemic therapy: views problems as arising from relationship patterns, not individuals
- Emotionally Focused Therapy (EFT): helps couples identify and change negative interaction cycles
- Structural family therapy: restructures family organization and boundaries
- Gottman method: research-based approach to strengthening relationships

## Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT)

- Psychological flexibility: being present, open to experience, and doing what matters
- Acceptance: allowing difficult thoughts and feelings without trying to eliminate them
- Cognitive defusion: seeing thoughts as mental events rather than literal truths
- Values clarification: identifying what truly matters and committing to value-guided action
- Effective for chronic pain, anxiety, depression, substance abuse

## Group Therapy

- Multiple clients meet together with one or more therapists
- Benefits: social support, normalization, learning from others, practicing social skills
- Types: psychoeducational groups, process groups, support groups, skills training groups
- Particularly effective for substance abuse, social anxiety, grief, and trauma
