# Sleep and Dreams

## What is Sleep

Sleep is a naturally recurring state of reduced consciousness, decreased sensory activity, and inhibited voluntary muscle movement. Humans spend approximately one-third of their lives sleeping. Sleep is essential for physical health, cognitive function, and emotional well-being.

## Sleep Stages

Sleep cycles through distinct stages approximately every 90 minutes:

- Stage 1 (N1): light sleep, transition from wakefulness, lasts 1-5 minutes
- Stage 2 (N2): deeper sleep, heart rate slows, body temperature drops, lasts 10-25 minutes
- Stage 3 (N3): deep slow-wave sleep, difficult to awaken, body repairs tissues and strengthens immune system
- REM sleep: rapid eye movement, brain activity similar to waking, vivid dreams occur, muscles are paralyzed

Adults typically experience 4-6 complete cycles per night. Deep sleep dominates early cycles; REM sleep increases in later cycles.

## Why We Sleep

Sleep serves multiple critical functions:

- Memory consolidation: transferring information from short-term to long-term memory
- Brain waste clearance: the glymphatic system removes toxic metabolic byproducts during sleep
- Physical restoration: growth hormone released during deep sleep repairs tissues
- Immune function: sleep deprivation impairs immune response
- Emotional regulation: REM sleep processes emotional experiences

## Dreams

- Most vivid dreams occur during REM sleep
- Everyone dreams, though not everyone remembers their dreams
- Average person has 3-5 dreams per night, totaling about 2 hours
- Dream content often incorporates recent experiences and emotional concerns
- Lucid dreaming: awareness that one is dreaming while still asleep

## Sleep Disorders

- Insomnia: difficulty falling or staying asleep (affects 10-30% of adults)
- Sleep apnea: repeated breathing interruptions during sleep, causing daytime fatigue
- Narcolepsy: sudden uncontrollable episodes of falling asleep
- Restless leg syndrome: uncomfortable sensations causing an urge to move the legs
- Sleepwalking (somnambulism): performing complex behaviors while asleep

## Sleep Deprivation

Chronic sleep deprivation (less than 7 hours for adults) is associated with:

- Impaired cognitive function and decision-making
- Increased risk of obesity, diabetes, and cardiovascular disease
- Weakened immune system
- Depression and anxiety
- Microsleeps: brief involuntary episodes of sleep lasting seconds
