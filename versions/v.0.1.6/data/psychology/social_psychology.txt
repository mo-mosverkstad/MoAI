# Social Psychology

## Definition

Social psychology studies how people's thoughts, feelings, and behaviors are influenced by the actual, imagined, or implied presence of others. It examines how social situations shape individual behavior.

## Conformity

People tend to align their behavior with group norms:

- Asch conformity experiments (1951): participants gave obviously wrong answers to match the group (37% conformed)
- Informational influence: conforming because we believe others have better information
- Normative influence: conforming to be accepted and avoid rejection
- Factors increasing conformity: group size, unanimity, public responses, collectivist culture

## Obedience

Stanley Milgram's experiments (1963) showed that ordinary people would administer apparently lethal electric shocks when instructed by an authority figure. 65% of participants delivered the maximum 450-volt shock. Factors increasing obedience: proximity of authority, distance from victim, institutional legitimacy.

## Bystander Effect

The more people present during an emergency, the less likely any individual is to help:

- Diffusion of responsibility: each person assumes someone else will act
- Pluralistic ignorance: people look to others for cues and interpret inaction as evidence that help isn't needed
- Kitty Genovese case (1964): widely reported (though later disputed) as an example of bystander apathy

## Attitudes and Persuasion

- Cognitive dissonance (Festinger, 1957): discomfort from holding contradictory beliefs motivates attitude change
- Elaboration Likelihood Model: persuasion occurs through central route (careful thinking) or peripheral route (superficial cues)
- Foot-in-the-door: agreeing to a small request increases compliance with a larger one
- Door-in-the-face: refusing a large request increases compliance with a smaller one

## Group Dynamics

- Groupthink (Janis): desire for harmony leads to poor decisions (Bay of Pigs, Challenger disaster)
- Social loafing: individuals exert less effort in groups than alone
- Deindividuation: loss of self-awareness in groups can lead to impulsive behavior
- Group polarization: group discussion tends to push opinions toward extremes

## Prejudice and Discrimination

- Stereotypes: generalized beliefs about group members
- Prejudice: negative attitudes toward a group
- Discrimination: negative behavior toward group members
- Implicit bias: unconscious associations that affect judgment
- Contact hypothesis (Allport): intergroup contact under equal conditions reduces prejudice
