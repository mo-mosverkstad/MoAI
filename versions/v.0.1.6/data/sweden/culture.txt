# Swedish Culture

## Values and Society

Swedish culture is characterized by several distinctive values:

- Lagom: "just the right amount" — moderation and balance in all things
- Jantelagen (Law of Jante): don't think you're better than anyone else — emphasis on equality and humility
- Fika: the Swedish coffee break tradition — a social ritual of coffee and pastries, taken seriously in workplaces and homes
- Consensus: decisions made through discussion and agreement rather than confrontation
- Gender equality: Sweden consistently ranks among the most gender-equal countries (generous parental leave shared between parents)
- Work-life balance: standard 40-hour work week, 5 weeks paid vacation, generous parental leave (480 days shared between parents)

## Traditions and Holidays

- Midsommar (Midsummer): celebrated on the Friday closest to June 24. Dancing around a maypole, flower wreaths, herring, strawberries, and schnapps. The most Swedish of all holidays.
- Lucia (December 13): festival of light during the darkest time of year. A girl wearing a crown of candles leads a procession singing "Sankta Lucia."
- Crayfish party (Kräftskiva): August tradition of eating crayfish outdoors with singing, paper hats, and aquavit
- Julbord: Christmas buffet with ham, meatballs, herring, Jansson's temptation (potato gratin), and rice pudding
- Valborg (Walpurgis Night, April 30): bonfires and singing to welcome spring

## Food

Traditional Swedish cuisine:

- Köttbullar (meatballs): served with cream sauce, lingonberry jam, and potatoes
- Smörgåsbord: buffet of cold and hot dishes (herring, salmon, meatballs, cheeses)
- Surströmming: fermented herring (extremely pungent, eaten in northern Sweden)
- Knäckebröd: crisp bread, a staple since Viking times
- Kanelbullar (cinnamon buns): celebrated on October 4 (Kanelbullens dag)
- Gravlax: cured salmon with dill and mustard sauce
- Semla: cardamom bun filled with almond paste and whipped cream (eaten before Lent)

## Literature and Film

- August Strindberg (1849-1912): playwright and novelist (Miss Julie, The Red Room)
- Selma Lagerlöf (1858-1940): first woman to win the Nobel Prize in Literature (1909)
- Astrid Lindgren (1907-2002): children's author (Pippi Longstocking, sold 165 million books)
- Stieg Larsson (1954-2004): The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo (Millennium series)
- Ingmar Bergman (1918-2007): film director (The Seventh Seal, Wild Strawberries, Persona)
- ABBA: pop group, one of the best-selling music acts of all time (400 million records)
