# Swedish Economy and Industry

## Overview

Sweden has a highly developed export-oriented economy with a GDP of approximately $590 billion (2023), making it the largest economy in Scandinavia. It combines a comprehensive welfare state with a competitive market economy, often called the "Nordic model."

## Key Statistics

- GDP per capita: approximately $55,000 (among the highest in the world)
- Population: approximately 10.5 million
- Unemployment rate: approximately 7-8%
- Currency: Swedish krona (SEK)
- Major trading partners: Germany, Norway, Denmark, Finland, United States, United Kingdom

## Major Industries

- Automotive: Volvo Cars, Volvo Trucks, Scania (trucks and buses)
- Telecommunications: Ericsson (5G infrastructure, mobile networks)
- Furniture and retail: IKEA (world's largest furniture retailer, founded 1943)
- Fashion: H&M (second-largest clothing retailer globally)
- Music and streaming: Spotify (founded in Stockholm, 2006)
- Gaming: Mojang (Minecraft), King (Candy Crush), DICE (Battlefield)
- Defense: Saab (Gripen fighter jet, submarines)
- Pharmaceuticals: AstraZeneca (co-headquartered in Sweden)
- Mining: LKAB (Europe's largest iron ore producer), Boliden (metals)
- Paper and packaging: SCA, Stora Enso

## The Swedish Model

The Nordic/Swedish model combines:

- Free market capitalism with strong competition
- High taxes (tax-to-GDP ratio ~44%, among the highest in the world)
- Comprehensive welfare state (universal healthcare, education, parental leave)
- Strong labor unions and collective bargaining (approximately 70% union membership)
- Open trade policy (export-dependent economy)
- High levels of trust and social cohesion

## Innovation

Sweden consistently ranks among the world's most innovative countries:

- Inventions: dynamite (Nobel), ball bearing (SKF), adjustable wrench, pacemaker, Tetra Pak, three-point seatbelt (Volvo), Bluetooth, Spotify
- R&D spending: approximately 3.4% of GDP (among the highest globally)
- Startup ecosystem: Stockholm produces more billion-dollar startups per capita than any city except Silicon Valley
