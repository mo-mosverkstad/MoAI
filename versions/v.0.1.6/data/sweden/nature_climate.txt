# Swedish Nature and Climate

## Geography

Sweden is the largest country in Scandinavia and the fifth-largest in Europe by area (450,295 km²). It stretches 1,572 km from north to south, spanning from 55°N to 69°N latitude.

- Northern Sweden (Norrland): vast boreal forests, mountains along the Norwegian border, midnight sun in summer and polar night in winter
- Central Sweden (Svealand): mix of forests, lakes, and agricultural land. Contains Stockholm and Uppsala.
- Southern Sweden (Götaland): more temperate, agricultural plains (Skåne), mixed forests

## Climate

Sweden's climate varies dramatically from south to north:

- Southern Sweden: temperate maritime climate. Winters: -3 to 3°C. Summers: 15-25°C.
- Central Sweden: continental climate. Winters: -5 to -15°C. Summers: 15-22°C.
- Northern Sweden (above Arctic Circle): subarctic climate. Winters: -15 to -30°C. Summers: 10-18°C.
- Midnight sun: above the Arctic Circle, the sun doesn't set for weeks around midsummer (June)
- Polar night: above the Arctic Circle, the sun doesn't rise for weeks around midwinter (December)

## Forests and Lakes

- Forests cover 69% of Sweden's land area (28 million hectares) — mostly spruce and pine
- Sweden has approximately 100,000 lakes (Vänern is the largest in the EU: 5,655 km²)
- Allemansrätten (Right of Public Access): everyone may walk, ski, or camp on any land (including private) as long as they don't disturb or damage
- 30 national parks protect diverse landscapes from alpine tundra to old-growth forest

## Wildlife

- Moose (elk): approximately 300,000-400,000 in Sweden, national symbol
- Brown bear: approximately 2,800 (largest population in Scandinavia)
- Wolf: approximately 400 (controversial — conflicts with reindeer herding)
- Lynx: approximately 1,200
- Arctic fox: endangered, approximately 300 in Scandinavian mountains
- Reindeer: approximately 250,000 (semi-domesticated, herded by Sami people)
- White-tailed eagle: recovered from near-extinction, now approximately 1,000 breeding pairs

## Northern Lights (Aurora Borealis)

The aurora borealis is visible in northern Sweden (Lapland) from September to March. It is caused by charged particles from the Sun interacting with Earth's magnetic field and atmosphere, producing green, purple, and red curtains of light. Abisko National Park is one of the best viewing locations in the world due to its clear skies.
