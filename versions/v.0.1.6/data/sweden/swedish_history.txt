# Swedish History

## Viking Age (793-1066)

Sweden's Vikings (known as Varangians) primarily traveled east, establishing trade routes to Constantinople and Baghdad along Russian rivers. They founded the Kievan Rus' state and served as elite guards (Varangian Guard) for the Byzantine Emperor. Swedish Vikings traded furs, amber, and slaves for silver, silk, and spices.

## Medieval Period (1066-1523)

- 1008: King Olof Skötkonung becomes the first Christian king of Sweden
- 1164: Uppsala becomes an archbishopric
- 1252: Stockholm founded (traditionally by Birger Jarl)
- 1397: Kalmar Union unites Sweden, Denmark, and Norway under one monarch
- 1520: Stockholm Bloodbath — Danish King Christian II executes 80-90 Swedish nobles

## Swedish Empire (1611-1721)

Sweden became a major European power under the Vasa dynasty:

- Gustav II Adolf (1611-1632): transformed Sweden into a military superpower, intervened decisively in the Thirty Years' War
- 1648: Peace of Westphalia grants Sweden territories in northern Germany
- Swedish Empire at its peak controlled Finland, Estonia, Latvia, and parts of Germany and Poland
- Charles XII (1697-1718): lost the Great Northern War to Russia, ending Swedish imperial ambitions
- 1721: Treaty of Nystad — Sweden cedes Baltic territories to Russia

## Modern Sweden (1809-present)

- 1809: Sweden loses Finland to Russia (had been part of Sweden for 600 years)
- 1814: Sweden gains Norway in a personal union (dissolved peacefully in 1905)
- 1814-present: Sweden has not participated in any war (over 200 years of peace)
- 1930s-1970s: development of the Swedish welfare state (folkhemmet — "the people's home")
- 1995: Sweden joins the European Union
- 2024: Sweden joins NATO, ending over 200 years of military non-alignment

## Swedish Neutrality

Sweden maintained neutrality through both World Wars, though this was controversial:

- World War I: remained neutral, profited from trade with both sides
- World War II: officially neutral but made concessions to Nazi Germany (iron ore exports, transit of German troops to Norway)
- Cold War: armed neutrality with strong defense forces
- Post-Cold War: active in UN peacekeeping, EU membership, partnership with NATO
