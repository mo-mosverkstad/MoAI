# Uppsala

## Overview

Uppsala is Sweden's fourth-largest city with approximately 177,000 inhabitants. Located 70 km north of Stockholm, it is one of the oldest cities in Scandinavia and has been a center of religion, education, and culture for over a thousand years.

## Uppsala University

Uppsala University, founded in 1477, is the oldest university in Scandinavia and one of the oldest in Northern Europe. It consistently ranks among the top 100 universities worldwide.

Notable alumni and faculty:

- Carl Linnaeus (1707-1778): father of modern taxonomy, developed binomial nomenclature for classifying organisms
- Anders Celsius (1701-1744): astronomer who created the Celsius temperature scale
- Svante Arrhenius (1859-1927): Nobel Prize in Chemistry (1903), first to predict CO₂-driven global warming
- Dag Hammarskjöld (1905-1961): UN Secretary-General, Nobel Peace Prize (posthumous, 1961)

The university has 8 Nobel Prize laureates associated with it and approximately 45,000 students.

## History

- Viking Age: Old Uppsala (Gamla Uppsala) was a major religious and political center with royal burial mounds dating to the 5th-6th centuries
- 1164: Uppsala becomes the seat of the Archbishop of Sweden
- 1477: Uppsala University founded by Archbishop Jakob Ulfsson
- 1702: much of the city destroyed by fire
- 18th century: Linnaeus transforms Uppsala into a center of natural science

## Landmarks

- Uppsala Cathedral (Domkyrkan): the largest church in Scandinavia (118.7 meters tall). Built 1270-1435 in French Gothic style. Contains the relics of King Eric IX and the tomb of Carl Linnaeus.
- Uppsala Castle: 16th-century royal castle on a hill overlooking the city. Built by King Gustav Vasa.
- Gamla Uppsala: ancient burial mounds and the site of a pre-Christian temple
- Linnaeus Garden (Linnéträdgården): botanical garden restored to its 18th-century appearance
- Carolina Rediviva: university library housing the Silver Bible (Codex Argenteus, 6th century)

## Economy

Uppsala's economy is driven by education, research, biotechnology, and IT. The city hosts major pharmaceutical and biotech companies. Uppsala Science Park is one of Sweden's largest science parks with over 150 companies.
