# Malmo

Malmo is the third-largest city in Sweden, located in the southern region of Skane. It is connected to Copenhagen, Denmark via the Oresund Bridge, forming a major cross-border metropolitan area. Malmo is a key link connecting Sweden to continental Europe.

## Geography and Connection to Europe

Malmo is located at the southwestern tip of Sweden, directly across the Oresund strait from Copenhagen, Denmark. The Oresund Bridge, completed in the year 2000, is a combined road and rail bridge that connects Sweden to Denmark and thereby to continental Europe. This bridge is approximately 16 kilometers long and is one of the longest combined road and rail bridges in Europe. Through the Oresund Bridge and onward via Denmark, Sweden is connected to the European motorway and rail networks, providing direct land routes to Germany, the Netherlands, and the rest of continental Europe.

Sweden is also connected to continental Europe by ferry services from Gothenburg and Stockholm to Germany, Poland, and the Baltic states. Air connections from Stockholm Arlanda, Gothenburg Landvetter, and Malmo Sturup airports provide further links to all major European cities.

## Diversity and Culture

Malmo is known for its multicultural population and vibrant arts scene. Over 180 nationalities are represented in the city.

Landmarks:
- Turning Torso skyscraper
- Malmo Castle
- Mollevangen district

## Economy

Strong sectors include:
- Green energy
- ICT
- Logistics
- Creative industries
- Life sciences

## Sustainability

Malmo is a global leader in sustainable urban development, with eco-friendly districts such as Vastra Hamnen (Western Harbour). The city has ambitious goals for carbon neutrality and renewable energy.
